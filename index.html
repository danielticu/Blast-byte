Milestone 1

The problem

People have the need to be entertained for brief periods, wether on the subway, in the bus station, or at the restaurant while waiting for the order. There is the need for an entertaining game that can be played for just a few seconds or minutes and still progress. There are many available games which can do that but the average playing time is low and the global market is so big that there is still a big part of it ready for new products.

Our solution

A simple game which can entertain a user for a few minutes of his spare time. The game is divided into several levels with the same general purpose. It is made with no specific ending, each level being followed by a similar but harder one. The main difference from a more complex game is that a user can play it for a few seconds or a few minutes and still make progress.

Customer segment

Our game is designed to be suitable for everyone. It does not contain neither sounds or pictures that could be frightening for young children, nor bad language. We could assign for it a PEGI 3 age label.

Competition

Being a simple game made to fill one's spare time, we can take into account as competition any entertainment activity. On one hand, our main competitors consist in equally complex games, from the same categories as us. On the other hand, we also compete against more complex games which users play on the long term. Last but not least, streaming services like Youtube and Netflix keep the users from playing our game.

Our advantage over the competition

The game is catchy and it has no specific ending but it becomes harder and harder the more you progress. The game is created using elements from some of the best selling and the best rated games on the market. A player is able to customize the game items and the background using a wide range of skins.

Key metrics

  • Downloads, Installs and Retention Rate
  • Rating on mobile stores: App Store, Google Play and Huawei Store
  • DAU, MAU, and Stickiness
  • Average Session Length
  • Reported Bugs
  • Organic Growth
  • Purchases, ARPU

Cost structure

"R&D" has the largest share in our cost with 65% and "Sales & Marketing" has a share of about 27%. Our cost in "R&D" and "Sales & Marketing" are mainly from payroll. Excluding this expanses "Rent and Utilities" has the largest weight with about 47% followed by "Equipment" with 39,21%.

Revenue streams

  • In-app advertising: rewarded photos or videos
  • In-app purchases: different skins, energy boosters, extra lives, superpowers and so on

Milestone 2

The way that we identified the problem

Well, that's easy, it was a normal morning of November, far from these rough times. Me and Daniel were travelling to the university by the metro having a casual conversation as usual, when we noticed that, around us, there were many people who travel alone, bored and unhappy. Searching for some statistics about how people fill their travelling time or those short periods of free time, we observed that the majority of people are a little bit nostalgic about games that were most-played some years ago. Some of them moved to Social Media applications, however, we identified a problem here. Based on multiple research articles, depression and anxiety became a real threat for more and more people and these Social Media apps don't represent a solution for it. Moreover, some studies showed that in some cases simple entertaining games are more efficient than medical treatment.

Solution for the problem

We'd like to launch an easy-to-play game that can be played wherever and whenever. Our project is designed to deliver not only entertainment and a way to spend your free time, but also a solution for depression and anxiety through multiple options to play, solo or with your friends. We also intend to include many leaderboard systems to enhance the competitivity and a reward mechanism, so that the users to feel appreciated.

Customer discovery plan - overview

Our process for customer discovery involved many methods, but the most important two were the online questionnaire and interviews. In our study, we tried to engage people of various ages and different types of occupations.

Customer discovery plan - detailed process

Firstly, we wanted to know what a highschool student thought about a mobile game and what were his habits when it came to spending free time, gaming, game aquisition and so on. Let's listen together what our confident and smart guy, Razvan, has to say.

Razvan is passionate about computer games, especially first person shooters. As for smartphone games, his personal favorite is Among Us because of the multiplayer side of the game. He admits that the multiplayer functionality and the competition with friends is the main motivation for playing. Also, when having a little spare time he often uses social media as a form of entertainment.

Moreover, in order to validate our idea, we decided to construct an online questionnaire with questions that will help us both establish a focus group and see the needs and habits of that group. The biggest amount of players are in the under 18 groups, but the ones that spend the most amount of money on a game are in the 18-25 range. This means that we will have to develop the right product to suit both categories. Almost all of the participants are playing games on a smartphone instead of a tablet and that showed us on what platform to focus on our application.

Almost 30% of the people interviewed admitted to having paid money for game items and bonuses, with the majority of them paying under 100 RON per month. Almost half of all the items that out subjects bought are valued between 10 and 25 RON, all the other price ranges having an equal distribution. All our respondents agree that limited offers and one-time deals are the best way to make them purchase an item in the game.

When asked about a game's advertising and which ads were the most effective in reaching them, almost half of the survey's subjects agreed that the most effective way of advertising a game is through friend's recommendations. Those games are also the ones that were played for the longest amount of time. Most of the subjects admitted that they are more likely to recommend a game to a friend based on the ability to play together and on the overall positive experience of the game than based on invitation rewards.

When asked why they come back and play the game one more time, a quarter of all the respondents answered that boredom is the number one factor in pulling out their smartphone and continuing the game. In the second place stand the daily rewards and missions which can be lost if a day is skipped.

Insights that have affected our product

We were happy to find out that the majority of people between the ages of 3 and 25, of both genders, are frequently playing some kind of smarpthone game. This is how we established our focus group. After interpreting the results we found out that most people emjoy simple and entertaining games and feel the need for a new game on the market. The main reason for playing a game is boredom and after a few days playing, people return to the game mainly for daily rewards.

We were happy to see that wen asked the name of a smartphone game, most of the respondents named a small game like Temple Run, Candy Crush or Among Us.

We realised that in order to promote a game, a good practice is to buy ads in other games, so that players will see another game while they are already playing.

Ready for Customer Validation

We think that we are ready to move to the Customer Validation stage because our solution was validated by the people's opinions. As can be observed in the graphs, the majority of people choose to play a game to fill their free time and appreciate if that game has the option to play with friends, the main two criteria which we chose to build this product for.

    Milestone 5

    How we advertised our landing page


    In order to get a realistic feedback on advertising the landing page we decided to go for Google Ads Services. Our campaing lasted for 7 days and it resulted in 173 users getting on our landing page.

    Advertisment campaign stats:
  • Ad clicks (site visits): 173
  • Ad views: 6681
  • Campaing cost: 40 RON
  • Campaign duration: 7 days
  • Countries targeted: Romania, Serbia, Moldova
  • Medium CPC: 0.23 RON
  • CTR: 2.59%
  • Search key words: "game store", "buy pc games", "gaming computers", "card game", "video game", "video games", "card games"

    • Statistics


    Desktop and laptop

    • users who clicked "Send" button: almost 20%
    • users who clicked "Google Play" button: almost 10%
    • users who clicked "Game" button: almost 10%
    • users who clicked "Feedback" button: almost 5%
    • users who scrolled the entire page: more than 10%
    • users who scrolled the first half of the page: more than 25%

    Tablet

    • users who tapped on "Send" button: almost 30%
    • users who tapped on "Google Play" button: almost 25%
    • users who scrolled the entire page: more than 25%
    • users who scrolled the first half of the page: more than 35%

    Smartphone

    • users who tapped on "Send" button: more than 25%
    • users who tapped on "Google Play" button: almost 15%
    • users who scrolled the entire page: more than 5%
    • users who scrolled the first half of the page: more than 25%

    Feedback

    Out of 173 site visits we recieved only one feedback which advised us to install the app on iOS Emus, a free application store for iPhone.


    Subscriptions

    We received a total of 47 form subscriptions using Formspree. However, almost 20% were duplicated which means that the users expected a sumbission confirmation and pushed the send button multiple times. Also, we need to validate the form fields in order to allow only name and email completion.

    What did we observe?


    Concerning the heatmaps, we had very good results on Smartphone and Tablet users, but we noticed some random clicking regarding PC users. It was great that we included the subscription form in the upper part of the web page so that 100% of the users could see it. We had two calls to action, the subscription form and the Google Play and App Store download buttons. As for the scrolling, we need to take into consideration shortening our webpage and making it more attractive in order to make the users scroll all the way to the bottom. We had very good results with tablet users regarding the heatmaps, but there were only 20 page views from tablets.

    For PC we had an average of 3.5 clicks per user, for tablet we had about 1.1 clicks per user and fot smartphone 0.77 clicks per user. This means that the PC users do a lot of random clicking on the page and a lot of the smartphone users visit the site by accident.

    Advertisment campaign conclusion


    It cost us 40 RON to get 173 people on our landing page. About 10% of the users left the site in under 4 seconds so we can conclude that they clicked the ad by accident. Also, another 20% of the users stayed on the page for mote than 1 minute, which means that they left the browser tag open. According to Google Analytics, the average site visit was 13 seconds, but after removing the lower and upper limits we obtained an average of 20 seconds

    All in all we gathered 34 subscriptions and 25 clicks on the download buttons.

    Milestone 7

    Minimum viable product


    We uploaded the game on Google Play on the 29th of December and by the 2nd of January it was accepted and posted. The game was rated as a PEGI 3 app, which means that the game is suitable for all ages by containing no violence, or explicit content.

    The first screen of the game is the dashboard. The player can then begin the game or enter the Options pannel in order to toggle settings. When the game starts, a timer appears on the center of the screen along with a meteorite counter. The counter represents the number of metrorites destroyed by the player. With the passing of the time the game gets more difficult with more and more meteorites showing up on the screen. The game ends when 10 meteorites are on the screen. At the end the player can view a video ad in order to double the score, this action can only be done once.

    As for the revenue streams, we are displaying a skippable video ad after every game and a non-skippable ad when the player chooses to double the score. In order to change the background and the meteorites, the player must buy new skins from the Store section. In the MVP the players must view a non-skippable ad in order to obtain the skins.
